Exploration of Loan Data from Prosper¶

Investigation Overview¶

I chose the Prosper Loan Data to investigate on the structure of loans. I will investigate the relationship between loan pricing, rating and other variables.

Dataset Overview¶

In the dataset are 113937 loan entries. The loan data contains 81 columns, which is a significant amount of features assosicated with each loan.

n the dataset are 113937 loan entries. The loan data contains 81 columns, which is a significant amount of features assosicated with each loan.

Most variables are numeric (float or int) like LoanOriginalAmount, BorrowerRate etc. A significant amount are ordinal values like the ProsperRating (lowest - 0 - N/A, 1 - HR, 2 - E, 3 - D, 4 - C, 5 - B, 6 - A, 7 - AA - highest) or the ProsperScore (from 1-10, with 10 being the highest and 1 the lowest risk score).

Some of the numeric values represent categorical data, e.g ListingCategory (numeric): 0 - Not Available, 1 - Debt Consolidation, 2 - Home Improvement, 3 - Business, 4 - Personal Loan, 5 - Student Use, 6 - Auto, 7- Other, 8 - Baby&Adoption, 9 - Boat, 10 - Cosmetic Procedure, 11 - Engagement Ring, 12 - Green Loans, 13 - Household Expenses, 14 - Large Purchases, 15 - Medical/Dental, 16 - Motorcycle, 17 - RV, 18 - Taxes, 19 - Vacation, 20 - Wedding Loans.

Distribution of Loan Amounts¶

Majority of the loans are between 1000 and 15000 USD with a slightly right skewed distribution, however there are clearly spikes at 4000, 10000 and 15000 USD.

Interest Rate vs Rating¶

The interest rate decreases step by step with every rating category increase. The worst rating category (1) shows a mean interest rate of 32%. The best rating category (7) shows a mean interest rate of 8%.

Interest Rate vs Loan Amount vs. Rating¶

The correlation between interest rate and loan amount is negative and highly dependent on the rating category. There is supposed to be a causality, as banks should be unlikely giving big loans to customers with bad ratings, so in turn customers with better rating receive bigger loans and the better ratings lead to lower interest rates for these loans in general. Additionally, there are almost no rates below 5% (supposedly a floor to cover administartive costs).

Further Influential Factors on Interest Rate¶

Other factors, that influence interest rate are the term of the loan, as well as the loan repayment amount. The mean interest rate for 12-month loans is slightly lower (15%) than for long-term loans (17%). As the interest rate increases, also the monthly loan payments are likely to increase, however only few monthly loan payments are above 800 USD.

Finally, the interest rate also varies from month to month. The mean interest rate was the highest from November to January. Consequently, the best month to get a loan is April.

Influencial Factors on Rating Category Part I¶

Debt to income ratio is strongly correlated with the rating category, as well as the number of delinquencies. If the borrower was a homeowner, the rating was likely to be better. Also, if the borrower has not utilised the bankcard before. A full time employment is beneficial for a good rating (as "Part-Time" and "Retired" was only slightly better on the rating scala compared to "Not Employed"). And finally,the income also influences the rating category.

An interesting fact is that the rating gives priority to debt ratio over delinquencies, as the category with the lowest rating (1) had lower deliquiency rates compared to the second lowest deliqency rates.

Development of Loan Amounts and Interest Rate over Years¶

From 2005 to 2009, the mean loan amounts and also the mean interest rates significantly increased. The mean loan amount in 2005 was approx. 2500 USD, in 2014, however, the mean loan amount was approx. 8000 USD. The mean interest rate in 2005 was approx. 9%, and in 2014 it rose up to approx. 20%.
